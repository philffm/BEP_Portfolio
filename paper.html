Week 1: Low Fidelity Prototyping on Paper

1.1. Introduction

The subject about our first assignment dealt all about small screens and was meant to apply our just gained low-fidelity paper prototyping skills to a new context: A small screen. To get more specific: We had to create a quick paper prototype for a fitness tracker app, which should be both applicable for the smartphone, but also the users’ smartwatch.

Essential functions as mentioned in the assignment were:

  1. a start – and stop button
  2. track burned calories, travel distance and current speed
  3. real-time information display
  4. daily/weekly/monthly view

1.2. The first draft

I decided, to implement every essential function, but instead of needing the user to manually start an activity, to automatically detect the users’ activity while displaying live data and giving the opportunity of either tagging the current activity from when it was first detected or to reset the current activity.

1.3. Feedback session

Within the assignment, we conducted a feedback session with at least one fellow student.

Sample size: magical number of 3 people

In the first instance, I advised my user, to just start with the application (without giving any advice. However, he was not that sure about, where to start and on how the tagging-feature relates to the live information. As well, users felt irritated by the large “swipe-down”-area, which leads to daily, weekly and monthly views. As well, they noted, that there was not yet a way to pair an accessory.

1.4. Improvements: Prototypes after the feedback

After creating actionable steps derived from the user feedback, I immediately started with applying the gained insights in yet another rapid paper prototyping session. The outcomes were as follows:

  • Smaller swipe-area (don't put users at risk, to accidently swipe down)
  • Elaborate on live data as mentioned in the assignment (color, coded, largest space)
  • Decluttered the interfaces for a clearer navigation
  • Added a Setting-page where users can pair/unpair their devices
  • Encourage use of icons instead of text in the smart watch screen

1.5. Reflective conclusion: Lo-fi-paper prototyping

Without any doubt: This assignment got my relationship towards paper prototyping to a completely new level.  Mainly I was impressed by the fact, how fast it  suddenly was possible to design for 10 minutes,  test another 10 minutes and design again. The things we tested were the most simple prototypes one might think of, that still contained all of the ideas covered in the ideation process/requirements.

In the end, experiences and usability tests gave me way more actionable insights than I expected from this assignment. That's when I realized  the importance of conducting lo-fi prototypes  was completely new to me - or maybe I  underestimated the topic before as I never used to like paper & hand writing tasks. But for the sake of valuable insights in the early development process, this part will definitely play a more certain role in my future projects.  Let me give an example: In Project for Bespoke Design, we focussed a lot on our end-result and that's where a gross part f the creative process took place. What I back then might perceived as normal iteration steps within the hi-fi prototype, probably made things more complicated than needed. My learnings: Invest time in lo-fi: A solid framework is the essence of every bigger (construction) project. (Just take the Berlin Airport,  which takes forever to be finished as things went wrong from the beginning).
